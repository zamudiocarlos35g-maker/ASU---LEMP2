The "Tactile & Teamwork" Classroom

This plan is designed to foster independence, safety, and a strong sense of community. My philosophy is built on my hands-on experience as a paraeducator for students with visual impairments.

Core Philosophy:

  • ✓ Foster Independence
  • ✓ Develop Critical Thinking
  • ✓ Build Strong Self-Advocacy Skills

The Power of Predictability

Clear, consistent routines are essential for predictability and safety. For students with visual impairments, we build routines around non-visual cues: spatial memory, auditory signals, and tactile guides.

Daily Routines

  • Arriving at School: Locate assigned tactile square, store backpack on labeled hook, and begin Morning Sensory Task.
    Auditory Cue: A 3-note chime signals the start.
  • Turning in Work: Locate "Done" tray using its unique texture marker.
    Auditory Cue: Soft verbal affirmation ("Thank you").

Movement & Transitions

  • Transitioning: Listen for the "Transition Song" (15s jingle). Clear desk, stand, and push chair in.
    Cue: The jingle and the physical 'click' of the chair.
  • Line Up / Hallway: Line up next to the tactile rope guide on the wall. Walk with two fingers on the rope.
    Cue: The rope is the primary navigation tool.

Classroom Flow

  • Requesting Bathroom/Water: Raise hand silently. Place non-writing hand flat on the desk as a tactile self-regulation cue.
  • Material Distribution: Materials are placed directly into students' hands or in the same desk spot every time.
  • Fast Finishers: Choose from the Fast Finisher Center (Braille/audio books, tactile puzzles, sensory bins).

Our Classroom System

We use a clear and positive system for rules, reinforcement, and consequences. The goal is always to model, practice, and re-teach.

Our 5 Rules

  1. Be Respectful
    (Use Kind Words and Actions)
  2. Be Responsible
    (Care for Our Tools and Space)
  3. Be Ready
    (Follow Directions Quickly)
  4. Be Safe
    (Keep Your Body Secure)
  5. Be a Team Player
    (Help Others Learn)

Positive Reinforcement

  • 👍
    Teacher Attention

    Frequent, specific, and genuine praise.

  • ⭐
    Public Recognition

    Celebrating the "Braille Star" over announcements.

  • 🎓
    Certificates & Tokens

    Weekly awards for meeting specific goals.

  • 🎉
    Special Activities

    Low-cost, high-interest privileges (e.g., "Lunch with the Teacher").

Consequence Hierarchy

Delivered privately and calmly. The goal is always re-teaching.

A quiet tap on the desk or a private, quiet reminder. This is the least intrusive cue.

Student misses 5 minutes of choice time. The behavior is logged by the teacher.

Student completes a Behavior Re-teaching Form (large print/braille). **Contact with parent/guardian.**

Reserved for serious safety issues. **Immediate parent contact** and documented action.
